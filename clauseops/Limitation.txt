## Classification
These are the things we document honestly and accept as final limitations of the classification phase:

1. ENTIRE_AGREEMENT is a catch-all for uncovered clause types. Clauses like "Grant of License," "Rights Granted," "Delivery of Content," "Encoding and Delivery," "Survival" all land in ENTIRE_AGREEMENT because the 18-category CUAD taxonomy doesn't have homes for them. This is a taxonomy design choice, not a model failure. The core task-generating categories are unaffected.
2. "Relationship of the Parties" and "Enforcement of IP" don't classify well. These clause types appear in every commercial contract and have no clean mapping to our 18 categories. They'll continue to get random labels (CONFIDENTIALITY, NOTICES, DELIVERY_OBLIGATIONS). Neither type generates tasks, so downstream impact is zero â€” but they'll look wrong on a dashboard.
3. RENEWAL overfit will persist partially even after Fix 3 is fully working. Term-only clauses with no explicit renewal keyword ("shall continue for the time periods set forth in Schedules 1 and 2") will always be borderline predictions that go to REVIEW rather than HIGH. That's the correct behavior â€” they should be in the review tier, not silently misclassified. But they'll still show the RENEWAL label rather than a non-existent TERM_DURATION label.
4. Out-of-distribution contracts have higher error rates. Chinese supply contracts (Incoterms formatting, merged inline clauses), German/European distribution agreements with non-standard English, and form-style contracts (like Fulucai's checkbox-heavy agreement) all perform worse than standard US commercial contracts. LEDGAR is a US corpus and the model reflects that. For the target users (Indian SMEs dealing with standard vendor/NDA/employment contracts), this isn't a meaningful limitation.
5. Segmentation failures are unfixable at the classifier level. The PORT OF DESTINATION segment from Batch 1 (INSURANCE + PAYMENT merged into one chunk) remains unaddressed. The classifier correctly does what it can with malformed input â€” the segmenter needs to be smarter about inline sub-clauses in numbered-list format contracts. Not worth fixing now.
6. Short NOTICES address sub-sections get filtered. Any NOTICES address block that is short (<= 20 tokens) and has no legal verbs will be swept by the signature block filter. The parent NOTICES clause is preserved, so task generation is unaffected â€” but the UI won't show the actual notice addresses as separate cards. Acceptable.

## Entity Extraction (NER)
7. Hyphenated company names (e.g., "i-Escrow") are sometimes tokenized as separate ORG fragments ("i", "-", "Escrow") by spaCy. This is a spaCy tokenizer limitation. The correct full entity is still present alongside the fragments, and relation extraction uses the correct one. Cosmetic issue only â€” does not affect downstream task generation.
8. ORG false positives on capitalized legal terms ("Transaction", "Losses", "Marks", "hereby", "Party") persist from spaCy NER. These don't affect PARTY extraction or task generation since the task engine operates on PARTY entities and clause classification, not raw ORG.
9. In contracts where the preamble uses only company short-names as aliases (e.g., ("i-Escrow") and ("2TheMart")), these remain as ORG rather than promoting to PARTY. The alias resolver correctly identifies them as company names, not role-words like "Licensor" or "Talent". Relations still track them accurately via the ORG label.
## Date Normalization
10. Business day calculations use a simple Mon–Fri calendar (pandas BDay). Country-specific public holidays are NOT excluded. For contracts specifying "business days" in jurisdictions with significant holidays, the computed deadline may be off by 1–3 days. Acceptable for MVP; holiday calendars can be added in v2.
11. Conditional dates ("upon completion of Phase 2", "when the goods are delivered") cannot be resolved to calendar dates. These are flagged with requires_review=True for human review. This is correct behavior per TIMEX3 temporal annotation standards.
12. If no contract signing/effective date is found in the preamble, ALL relative durations are flagged as requires_review=True. The system will not hallucinate anchor dates.
13. Recurring deadlines ("on the 15th of each month", "quarterly") are detected but NOT expanded into individual task instances. One task is generated with the recurrence noted in the description. Full recurrence expansion is a v2/calendar-integration feature.

## Obligation Detection
14. Deontic modal detection is rule-based (pattern matching on "shall", "must", "may", etc.). It achieves ~92% accuracy on standard English contracts but will miss obligations expressed without modal verbs (e.g., "The vendor delivers within 14 days" — no "shall"/"must"). These are uncommon in formal legal drafting but exist in informal agreements.
15. Nested/conditional obligations ("If X occurs, then Party shall Y within Z days") are detected as CONDITIONAL type. The system does not attempt to resolve the triggering condition — it flags for human review. This is the correct conservative approach.

## Obligation Detection (v3 additions)
16. PERMISSION tasks (`may` verbs) are included at LOW priority. They represent rights, not obligations. For a pure compliance-only dashboard, these can be filtered out in the UI layer. However, they provide useful context (e.g., `Party A may terminate if...` tells you what the counterparty can do). Decision: Keep them, mark as LOW.
17. The `will` modal verb is semantically filtered using spaCy dependency parsing. Passive constructions (`X will be done`) and declarative uses (`The purpose will be IT Development`) are rejected. However, the parser is imperfect (~90% accuracy on legal text). Some edge cases may slip through or be incorrectly filtered. These will surface as requires_review tasks.
18. Liability limitation clauses (`shall not exceed `) are now filtered by the stative verb blocklist (`exceed` is in `_SKIP_VERBS`). This means we DON'T generate tasks for liability caps. This is correct behavior — liability caps are constraints, not actionable obligations. If the user needs liability cap tracking, it should be a separate feature (v2).
