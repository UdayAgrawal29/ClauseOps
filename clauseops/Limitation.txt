PART A — EARLIER LIMITATIONS (pre-rebuild)  [with current status]
==============================================================================

--- Segmentation ---
A1. Purely visual headings (bold-only / underlined / indentation-only) that Docling
    also misses are not detected.                                  STATUS: STILL APPLIES.
A2. Section-level sub-clauses inside a numbered-list contract were not split; whole
    Articles merged into one chunk ("the segmenter needs to be smarter about inline
    sub-clauses ... not worth fixing now").                        STATUS: FIXED (M1, Phase A
    — provision splitter for Section/Article/Clause markers).

--- Classification ---
A3. ENTIRE_AGREEMENT is a catch-all bucket; clauses like Grant of License, Delivery,
    Survival, Further Assurances land there because the 18-category taxonomy has no
    home for them.                                                 STATUS: TAXONOMY UNCHANGED,
    but it no longer drops obligations — classification is now ADVISORY, not a hard gate
    (M1, Phase B). The label may still look wrong on a dashboard.
A4. "Relationship of the Parties" / "Enforcement of IP" get arbitrary labels.  STILL APPLIES
    (cosmetic; these rarely generate tasks).
A5. RENEWAL overfit: term-only clauses with no renewal keyword stay borderline.  STILL APPLIES.
A6. Out-of-distribution contracts (non-US, non-English, form/checkbox) classify worse
    (LEDGAR is a US corpus).                                       STATUS: STILL APPLIES.
A7. Short NOTICES address sub-blocks get swept by the signature-block filter.  STILL APPLIES
    (parent NOTICES clause preserved; no task impact).

--- Entity Extraction (NER) ---
A8. Hyphenated company names ("i-Escrow") sometimes split into ORG fragments.  STILL APPLIES
    (cosmetic; correct full entity also present).
A9. ORG false positives on capitalized legal terms ("Transaction", "Losses").  STILL APPLIES
    (no PARTY/task impact).
A10. Short-name-only aliases stay ORG rather than promoting to PARTY.           STILL APPLIES.

--- Date Normalization ---
A11. Business-day math uses Mon-Fri only; country public holidays not excluded.  STILL APPLIES.
A12. Conditional dates ("upon completion of Phase 2") flagged requires_review, not
     resolved.                                                     STATUS: CORRECT BY DESIGN.
A13. If no anchor/effective date is found, all relative durations -> requires_review
     (no hallucinated anchors).                                   STATUS: CORRECT BY DESIGN.
A14. Recurring deadlines detected but NOT expanded into instances / a first concrete
     date.                                                         STATUS: STILL APPLIES.

--- Obligation Detection (original architecture) ---
A15. Deontic detection was rule/NLI based and MISSED obligations without a modal verb
     ("The vendor delivers within 14 days").                      STATUS: LARGELY FIXED for
     modal-less LISTS under "Obligations of {Party}" headings / "...as follows:" lead-ins
     (M3, Phase G). Free-flowing modal-less prose and tables still missed (see B1, B3).
A16. Nested/conditional obligations flagged CONDITIONAL, trigger not resolved.  CORRECT BY DESIGN.
A17. PERMISSION ("may") tasks were noise (~56% of output).         STATUS: FIXED — filtered by
     default (v4.1 config; still configurable).
A18. The "will" modal + liability caps ("shall not exceed") produced bad tasks.  STATUS:
     liability caps filtered; "will"/liability-disclaimer phrasing still occasionally
     misclassified (see B5).
A19. (Original v4 extraction) Zero-shot NLI + spaCy dependency parsing hallucinated
     parties (hardcoded "Contracting Party"), reversed logic, ~15% accuracy.    STATUS:
     FIXED — replaced by the custom modality classifier + offline extractive-QA extractor
     (grounded, cannot hallucinate). This was the Phase-4 rebuild.


==============================================================================
PART B — CURRENT LIMITATIONS (after Phase-4 rebuild + M1-M3 hardening)
==============================================================================
Validated by hand against 9 ground-truthed contracts (Co-Branding, Endorsement,
Affiliate, Development, Content License x3, Joint Venture x2).

Overall: production-viable for standard "shall"/"will"-drafted commercial contracts
(~85-95% recall & precision; deadlines mostly correct). The anti-hallucination grounding
invariant held on 100% of outputs (every extracted party/action is a substring of the
source clause). Weaker on table-based and passive-declarative drafting styles.

B1. TABLE-based duties: a duty-table MINER is now implemented (M4) — duty/
    responsibility/deliverable tables with a party in the first column are mined
    into "{party} shall {duty}" obligations (unit-tested). RESIDUAL: this only helps
    when the table SURVIVES segmentation. Docling sometimes drops or mangles a duty
    table before it reaches obligation detection (e.g. BORROWMONEY's "Member / Duties
    Description" matrix was lost in segmentation), so the real remaining bottleneck is
    table survival, not mining. Payment/royalty/contribution tables are intentionally
    NOT mined.

B2. Bulleted sub-duty lists under a single "... will include the following:" parent
    (e.g. a Manager-duties list a-e) partly collapse / are missed, because the bullets
    lack their own subject+modal and spaCy may merge them into the parent sentence.

B3. Passive operational obligations ("books ... will be kept", "funds will be placed",
    "distribution will be made") are now recovered (M4): rewritten to active voice with
    an agent taken from the section's governing party, an operational entity named in
    the sentence ("the Venture"), or a known organizational party. Every such task is
    flagged requires_review (the agent is inferred, not the grammatical subject, and the
    rewritten phrasing can be rough on complex sentences). RESIDUAL: passive clauses with
    no determinable operational entity ("Member meetings will be held quarterly") are
    still missed.

B4. QA self-referential / bad-agent spans on complex multi-party sentences
    ("Rogers shall pay to Rogers"; agent = "its sole expense ..."). Model-level; would
    need teacher-data regeneration to improve. Low frequency.

B5. Liability disclaimers ("Members will not be personally liable") occasionally
    misclassified as PROHIBITION by the modality classifier. Model-level precision.

B6. Passive/gerund action spans yield awkward (but content-correct) titles
    ("must not assigned", "must not developing"). Active-voice normalization deferred.

B7. Recurring deadlines: still flagged with a recurrence description but not expanded to
    a first concrete due_date (carried over from A14).

B8. Deadline labels ("Notice Period" / "Cure Period") are inferred and often imprecise;
    they appear only in the task description (titles no longer use them).

B9. Recall is contract-style dependent: infinitive-list JV ~78%, table/passive JV ~60%,
    standard shall/will contracts ~85-90%.

--- Inherited & by-design (still apply) ---
B10. OCR for scanned PDFs is intentionally deferred (not implemented).
B11. Business-day deadline math ignores public holidays (A11).
B12. No anchor date -> relative deadlines flagged requires_review, never guessed (A13).
B13. Out-of-distribution / non-US-English contracts perform worse (A6).
